Triangles

A triangle is a polygon that has three sides and three angles, but not just any three sides can form a triangle. The longest side must be shorter than the sum of the shorter two sides. The sides of a triangle always obey the Law of Cosines.

Circles

A circle can be seen as an infinitely-sided regular polygon. Once you know the radius or diameter of a circle, you can easily calculate its circumference and area. Lengths, areas, and angles of central portions of a circle are always in proportion.

Rectangle Perimeters

A rectangle is a four-sided polygon that has four right angles. If you have a limited perimeter to construct a rectangle, you will maximize the area of that rectangle by making it a square.

Rectangle Areas

A rectangle is a four-sided polygon that has four right angles. If you have a specific area that you wish to contain with an a rectangle, you will minimize the perimeter by making the rectangle a square.
